  • 1. Choosing and Listing Homes
    • Why do you need an agent to recommend listings?
      • Alternative: Do it yourself today.
        • Attend Open Houses.
        • Use Self-Help Open House Checklists.
          • 1. [ ] Roof condition and leaks
          • 2. [ ] Insulation: Many older houses lack insulation, leading to cold winters and hot summers
          • 3. [ ] Floor evenness and sounds: Uneven floors can indicate foundation problems or moisture-damaged wooden floors
          • 4. [ ] Door and window functionality: Doors that don't open smoothly may be affected by foundation issues
          • 5. [ ] Ceiling height and lighting: Many older homes in the Bay Area have low ceilings and inadequate lighting
          • 6. [ ] Orientation: Chinese buyers prefer south-facing homes, while Americans often prefer east-west orientations. Pay special attention to the orientation of the master bedroom and living room
          • 7. [ ] Floor plan: Check if the separation between active and quiet areas is reasonable and if the size of each room is appropriate
          • 8. [ ] Closet and storage space: Adequate storage space is important
          • 9. [ ] Garage: At least a two-car garage is preferred. Side-by-side is better than tandem, attached is better than detached, and front yard is better than backyard
          • 10. [ ] Street parking: A lot of street parking can indicate lower-income neighborhood
          • 11. [ ] Neighbor noise: Check if neighbors have pets, play musical instruments, or have motorcycles. Also check if there are preschools nearby
          • 12. [ ] Pets: Determine if the previous owner had pets as some people are allergic to animal hair
          • 13. [ ] Central air conditioning, heating, and fireplace
          • 14. [ ] Kitchen design: Do you like the design? Is there enough space? Consider L-shaped, T-shaped, U-shaped, and island layouts
          • 15. [ ] Bathrooms: Are the number and size of bathrooms adequate? Check for the presence of a shower, tub, and double sink
          • 16. [ ] T-section road: Cars' headlights can shine into the house, and there have been cases of cars accidentally crashing into houses
          • 17. [ ] Large trees: Roots of large trees in the yard can affect the house's foundation and damage water pipes
          • 18. [ ] Check areas hidden by flooring and furniture: The floor and walls may have damage
          • 19. [ ] Floor material: Solid wood > Engineered wood > AE > Laminate
          • 20. [ ] Foundation cracks
          • 21. [ ] Front and back yard landscaping: A beautiful yard is a plus
